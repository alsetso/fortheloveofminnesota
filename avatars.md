# For the Love of Minnesota — Connector Archetypes

## 1. Local Radio Hosts (Morning & Drive Time)
Trusted daily voices who shape community conversation.
- Reach broad, local audiences quickly
- Thrive on human-interest, participation-driven stories
- Ideal for simple call-to-action mentions

## 2. Neighborhood & City Facebook Group Admins
Gatekeepers of highly engaged local communities.
- Control trusted community spaces
- Posts spread rapidly through comments and shares
- High conversion from curiosity to participation

## 3. Local Instagram Community Page Owners
Visual storytellers of Minnesota life.
- Strong regional pride and identity
- Map screenshots perform extremely well
- Drive discovery and exploration behaviors

## 4. Weather Personalities
Unexpected but powerful community connectors.
- Deep trust across demographics
- Naturally tied to place and geography
- Perfect for seasonal and situational map use

## 5. Library Directors & Staff
Stewards of public knowledge and access.
- Seen as neutral, community-first leaders
- Aligned with public archives and shared memory
- Provide institutional credibility

## 6. Parks & Recreation Leaders
Champions of place-based experience.
- Directly connected to physical locations people love
- Strong seasonal engagement
- High relevance to outdoor and family audiences

## 7. Local Newsletter Writers & Editors
Curators of trusted local information.
- Built-in loyal readership
- Constant need for meaningful local content
- Excellent for soft launches and ongoing updates

## 8. Farmers Market & Community Event Organizers
Connectors of culture, food, and gathering.
- Face-to-face community influence
- Strong cross-demographic reach
- Natural fit for discovery and pride-driven mapping

## 9. Lake Association & Outdoor Group Leaders
Guardians of Minnesota’s natural identity.
- Deep emotional ties to specific places
- Strong stewardship mindset
- Encourage thoughtful, respectful participation

## 10. Bar Owners, Bartenders, & Coffee Shop Regulars
Hyper-local word-of-mouth engines.
- Daily conversations with diverse locals
- Informal trust and influence
- Powerful offline-to-online spread
